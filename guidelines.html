Guidelines

How our group worked together - process, standards, and reflections

This page documents the working guidelines our group agreed on at the start of the project and reflects on how well we followed them. It also covers our standards for academic integrity, accessibility, and web quality.

Group Working Guidelines

These guidelines were established in our first meeting and formalised in our Team Contract, which all three members signed before research work began.

Communication

  • All group communication happens through our shared group chat.
  • Members respond to messages within 24 hours on weekdays.
  • Major decisions require agreement from all three members.
  • If a member is unavailable, they notify the group at least 12 hours in advance.

Meetings

  • Meetings are held weekly, in-person on campus or online via Microsoft Teams.
  • An agenda is prepared and shared by Joudi at least 24 hours before each meeting.
  • Minutes are recorded and published to this page within 24 hours of each meeting.
  • All members come prepared, having completed their agreed tasks since the last meeting.

Work Quality and Review

  • All written content is reviewed by at least one other group member before publication.
  • In-text citations are included for every factual claim drawn from an external source.
  • All references are formatted in APA 7th edition and verified against the original source.
  • HTML is consistent across all pages - same navbar, header, and footer on every page.
  • No AI generated content is submitted as our own work without acknowledgement.

Website Standards and Accessibility

  • All pages use a consistent navigation bar linking to every section of the site.
  • All images include descriptive alt text for screen reader accessibility.
  • Colour contrast between text and background meets WCAG AA standards throughout.
  • Pages use semantic HTML elements - headings, lists, tables, figures - in the correct order.
  • The site is readable on both desktop and mobile screen sizes.
  • All external links include the full URL and were verified as functional at submission.
  • Each page includes an address element crediting the original template authors and our edits.

Group Reflection

Overall, our group worked well together throughout the project. Communication was consistent, we attended all three scheduled meetings without any absences, and the workload was divided in a way that played to each person's strengths. The one challenge we encountered was coordinating during a week when one member had reduced availability. We handled this by temporarily extending our response-time expectation to 48 hours, which worked well enough not to affect our deadlines.

Looking back, the thing we would change most is starting the reference sourcing earlier. Finding credible academic sources on a topic as recent as AI generated misinformation took longer than expected - several of the most relevant papers were published in 2023 or 2024 and were not always easy to access through standard library databases. We would also build the HTML structure before writing content next time, rather than doing both in parallel, to reduce the number of late formatting revisions needed.
