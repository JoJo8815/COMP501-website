Guidelines

How our group worked together — process, standards, and reflections

This page documents the working guidelines our group agreed upon at the start of the project, and reflects on how well we adhered to them. It also addresses academic integrity, accessibility, and web standards compliance relevant to this assignment.

Group Working Guidelines

These guidelines were established in our first meeting and formalised in our Team Contract.

Communication

  • All group communication occurs via our shared group chat (WhatsApp/Discord).
  • Members are expected to respond to messages within 24 hours on weekdays.
  • Major decisions (e.g., topic changes, submission format) require agreement from all three members.
  • If a member is unavailable, they notify the group at least 12 hours in advance.

Meetings

  • Meetings are held weekly, either in-person on campus or online via Microsoft Teams.
  • An agenda is prepared and shared by Joudi at least 24 hours before each meeting.
  • Minutes are recorded during the meeting and published to this website within 24 hours.
  • All members are expected to come prepared, having completed their agreed tasks.

Work Quality and Review

  • All written content is reviewed by at least one other group member before being published.
  • In-text citations must be included for every factual claim drawn from an external source.
  • All references must be formatted in APA 7th edition and verified against the original source.
  • HTML must be valid and consistent across all pages (same navbar, header, and footer).
  • No AI-generated content is submitted as our own work without acknowledgement.

Website Standards and Accessibility

  • All pages use a consistent navigation bar linking to every section of the site.
  • All images include descriptive alt text for screen reader accessibility.
  • Colour contrast between text and background meets WCAG AA standards throughout.
  • Pages use semantic HTML elements (headings, lists, tables, figures) appropriately.
  • The site is readable on both desktop and mobile screen sizes.
  • All external links open with a clear URL and are checked to be functional at time of submission.
  • Each page includes an address element crediting the original template authors and our edits.

Group Reflection

Overall, our group functioned well throughout the project. We maintained consistent communication, met all three scheduled meeting commitments, and divided the workload equitably. The main challenge we encountered was coordinating research across time zones during one member's travel, which we addressed by extending our response-time window to 48 hours for that week.

If we were to do this project again, we would begin sourcing references earlier in the process, as finding ten credible academic sources on a relatively recent topic proved more time-consuming than anticipated. We would also build the HTML structure before writing content, rather than doing both in parallel, to reduce the number of formatting revisions needed.
