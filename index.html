Deepfakes & AI-Generated Misinformation

Threats to Truth in the Digital Age

Welcome

This website presents our group research project for COMP501: Computing Technology in Society. Our topic is Deepfakes and AI-Generated Misinformation — one of the most pressing challenges posed by modern computing technology. Use the navigation bar above to explore each section of our research.

Abstract

Deepfakes are AI-generated videos, images, or audio clips that make a real person appear to say or do something they never actually did. Powered by deep learning techniques such as Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), these synthetic media have grown increasingly convincing and accessible. This project critically examines deepfakes and AI-generated misinformation by exploring the technology behind their creation, the opportunities they present, and the significant risks they pose to democratic processes, personal reputation, and public trust.

Using Taiwan's 2024 presidential election as a central case study, we document how deepfake videos and synthetic audio spread across social media platforms within hours, eroding voter trust before fact-checkers could respond. We explore the ethical and policy dimensions of this issue — examining who bears responsibility: AI developers, social media platforms, or governments — and evaluate the emerging choices available to society, including detection tools, platform regulation, and international legal frameworks.

Our research draws on a minimum of ten academic sources and aims to produce a balanced, evidence-based assessment of how society can respond to a technology that is already reshaping the information landscape.
